What is a boilerplate?

A boilerplate is the basic structure of a webpage, you can also think of it as a template. Every HTML page starts with a doctype declaration which just tells the browser what version of HTML it should render. HTML5 is the newest version. Example:
<!DOCTYPE html>

With our doctype in place it tells the browser what programming language we are using but now what people language, so lets add that in with
<DOCTYPE html>
<html lang="en">

</html>
"en" is the code for english, this is primarily used for improving accessibility of the webpage allowing for assitive technologies like screen readers to adapt accordingly to the language and use correct pronuncations. You may have noticed that there is a space between the 3rd and 5th lines, this is becasue everything we use to build our webpage needs to be inbetween the html tags.

Now we have our root element and language established we can move onto our head element, our head element is always our first element added under out html tag. The head element is where we put our important meta-information about our webpages, also important things required for our webpage to render correctly. No content on our webpage should be in our head element. Example:
<head> 

   <meta charset="UTF-8"
   <title>Browser Tab Name<title>
</head>
meta charset? UTF-8? title? What are all these things?? Well meta charset=UTF-8 is our meta tag with a character set or charset encoding of our webpage. We will get to what exactly the encoding is late on. Title is simpily what shows up in the Browser tab.

We have all of our meta information set up now we just need to actually put content on the webpage with a body element. Example: <body><body<
We will add to this later on but for now have a look at what a full boilerplate looks like.

<!DOCTYPE html>
<html lang="en">

   <head>
                <meta charset="UTF-8">
                <title>My First Website<title>
            </head>
                <body>
            *Content to be added*
            </body>
